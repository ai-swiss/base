# Your folder, your first document

*⏱ ~12 min · module 3/3, Discovery track*

**You will**: get your own BASE space, drop one of your real documents into it, and confirm it is genuinely being read, proven by the ✅ below.
**You need**: modules 1-2 completed.
↻ **Recap**: without looking, what changes the assistant's behavior? (editing its files)

1. Copy the `exemples/starter-perso` folder out of the repository, into your Documents, then
   rename it, for example `mon-assistant`. With the keyboard: right-click -> Copy -> Paste. Or
   ask your assistant: *"copy the starter-perso folder into my Documents"*.
2. Open THIS new folder in your tool (*File -> Open Folder*).
3. Drag one of your real documents into it (a procedure, a reference sheet). Safe formats: `.md`, `.txt`.
   If the format does not go through, retype the text into a new `.md` file.
4. Make three requests (the **interrogation protocol**):
   - a question that ONLY your document holds the answer to (it must cite the document);
   - a question foreign to your document (it must admit it does not know);
   - a request meant to route to one of your tasks.

✅ **Check**: answer 1 cites your document; answer 2 acknowledges that it does not know instead of inventing; answer 3 aims at the right intent. Taken together, these three proofs confirm that the import is real and that the assistant is not bluffing.

💡 **Why it worked**: a successful import is not "the file is there" but "the assistant uses it and stays honest about its limits". The interrogation protocol tests precisely that, without a single tool.

🔁 **At home**: which of your documents deserves to be the first real content in your assistant?

→ **And now**: you have completed the Discovery tour. To build for real (processes, evaluation, fieldwork), move on to the [Practitioner track](praticien-1-anatomie.md).

🆘 **Common breakdowns**: *It invents an answer to question 2*: that is the risk inherent to the *consigne* tier; deterministic routing and a good use_when ease it (Practitioner track). *It does not see your document*: make sure it is really INSIDE the open folder, and not somewhere else.
