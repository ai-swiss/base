# Learn BASE by Doing

**Your AI tool is the experience; Studio is the workshop.** This tutorial has you build, with
your own hands, an assistant for an imaginary tourist office (**Veytaux-les-Bains**) and then
bring it onto YOUR files. Every step ends with a check: you never move on without proof that
everything works. This verification is just one brick among others, serving what BASE really builds: a base you own and keep legible. The check rests on a deterministic test that you inspect and confirm yourself.

## Choose your starting point

### Discovery: 32 min once your tool is connected, nothing to install on the BASE side
You have never structured an AI's work. You open the finished tourist office, you talk to it,
you change a rule, you watch the assistant obey the file. **3 modules.**
By the end: you have your own folder, with one of your real documents inside it.
→ [Step 0: connect your tool](harnais.md) then [Module 1](decouverte-1-faites-le-parler.md).

### Practitioner: 122 min, Node installed
You want to build. You create your real project with `base init`, rebuild the tourist office
from fill-in-the-blank skeletons, add competences and templates to generate documents, evaluate
quality, handle a piece of field feedback, then migrate your own procedures. **9 modules.**
By the end: YOUR assistant answers on YOUR content and prepares your deliverables.
→ [Step 0](harnais.md) then [Module 1](praticien-1-anatomie.md).

### Team: 50 min
You are deploying for several people. Multi-scope workspace, egress governance over a real
confidential resource, distribution by git and MCP server. **3 modules.**
→ [Step 0](harnais.md) then [Module 1](equipe-1-workspace.md).

---

Each module shows an indicative duration (an estimate, not a stopwatch) and your progress; the
totals above are their sum. Stuck? Each module ends with the common failures and a comparison
point in `exemples/veytaux-tourisme`.
