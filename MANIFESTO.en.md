**Owning the structure of your expertise, in the age of AI.**

---

Generative AI changes a great deal, except one thing: it still does not know what you know. Your clients, your constraints, your risk threshold, your way of deciding, the history of your files: none of that lives in a model. A model masters countless domains but knows nothing of your reality; by default, it does not carry its memory from one session to the next, and the language that drives it stays underspecified, which is at once its flexibility and its fragility. With no written instructions, it improvises. Scattered across interfaces, those instructions become impossible to maintain.

The reflex inherited from SaaS platforms is to lock this collaboration inside closed spaces: hand-configured agents, instructions scattered across several screens, permissions strewn about, an agent choice you have to keep in your head. You produce faster, but you no longer know what guided the production. How then do you say what was verified, what remains reusable, what stays under human responsibility? AI weighs the mind down instead of lightening it.

BASE takes the opposite path.

The tipping point is simple. Producing now takes almost no effort; verifying remains costly. In most real work, no external verifier exists: no compiler, no schema, no rulebook. It falls to the human to spot and correct the errors, and to judge whether a response truly serves their intent, calibrated to the risk they accept. Everything else in this manifesto follows from that: keeping the human **able to verify**, without drowning them, and over time.

## What we hold to be true

1. **The durable value is neither the model nor the tool: it is the structure of your expertise.** Models change every six months; your business files, your processes, your rules and your decisions must remain yours.
2. **Text is authoritative.** Readable, versionable, portable Markdown. Neither a proprietary database, nor a format you could neither take with you nor audit.
3. **Generating takes little effort; verifying takes much.** Every AI output is to be held as a hypothesis, not a settled conclusion. A solid structure lightens verification, never the other way around.
4. **A guarantee is only real if it is enforced.** We honestly distinguish the instruction (a text, followed by a cooperative model) from the mechanism (enforced by the broker). We document this boundary instead of papering over it.
5. **The important decision stays human and visible.** Decision points, markers and the journal keep control and memory on the side of people.
6. **Sovereignty is not negotiable.** The local core of BASE lets nothing slip out by default. If you plug an AI tool, an embeddings provider or an API into it, you are the one who chooses what is entrusted to them. You remain free to switch tools; your files, they remain. The sovereignty that matters lies **around the models**: the freedom to articulate, to structure, to think with these intelligences.
7. **What matters must stay visible.** You should never have to go digging to reach what is essential. BASE puts forward what needs verifying or deciding, points the way rather than leaving you hanging, and keeps help within a word's reach. Burying what matters is already a small loss of verification.

## What we do not promise

BASE does not make AI infallible. It replaces neither IAM, nor DLP, nor legal archiving, nor the governance of a large organization. It imposes no model, no provider, no interface. It does not claim that everything must be automated, nor that AI possesses guaranteed intent or understanding.

Our ambition means to be **modest in its promises and strong in its method**: making collaboration between human and AI more structured, more verifiable and more durable. Its strength does not come from a promised reliability, but from the structure of the interaction and its safeguards: iterate, refocus, explain, and maintain human presence at the points that matter, to validate according to the accepted risk.

## Why it matters, at every scale

What holds for a freelancer holds, at larger scale, for a team, a school or a public administration. Real progress lies in **structuring the relationship** rather than delegating judgment: writing down what the system must know, bounding what it may do, keeping visible the decisions that commit you. You can then move forward with AI **without depending on a single platform**, and without giving up the structure of your work.

In a multilingual country, it is also a matter of linguistic comfort. The core of BASE is language-independent: the assistants you build work in German, French, Italian or English, in the language of those who use them. The framework's documentation starts in French; its translations form a commons to enrich.
